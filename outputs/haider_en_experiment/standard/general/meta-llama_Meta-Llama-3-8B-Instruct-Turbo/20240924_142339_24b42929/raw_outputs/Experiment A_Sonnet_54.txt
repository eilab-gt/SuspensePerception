Here is the annotation of the stanza:

1. Of this worlds theatre in which we stay, [Vitality]
2. My love like the spectator ydly sits [Vitality]
3. Beholding me that all the pageants play, [Vitality]
4. Disguysing diversly my troubled wits. [Uneasiness]
5. Sometimes I joy when glad occasion fits, [Beauty/Joy]
6. And mask in myrth lyke to a comedy: [Beauty/Joy]
7. Soone after when my joy to sorrow flits, [Sadness]
8. I waile and make my woes a tragedy. [Sadness]
9. Yet she, beholding me with constant eye, [Sadness]
10. Delights not in my merth nor rues my smart: [Sadness]
11. But when I laugh she mocks, and when I cry [Humor]
12. She laughs and hardens evermore her heart. [Sadness]
13. What then can move her? if nor merth nor mone, [Sadness]
14. She is no woman, but a senceless stone. [Sadness]
