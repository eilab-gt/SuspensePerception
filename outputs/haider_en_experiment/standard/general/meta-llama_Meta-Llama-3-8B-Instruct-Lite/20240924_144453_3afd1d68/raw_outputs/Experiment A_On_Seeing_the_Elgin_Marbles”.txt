Here is the annotation of the stanza:

1. My spirit is too weak—mortality Weighs heavily on me like unwilling sleep, [Sadness]
2. And each imagined pinnacle and steep Of godlike hardship tells me I must die [Sadness]
3. Like a sick eagle looking at the sky. [Sadness]
4. Yet ‘tis a gentle luxury to weep, [Beauty/Joy]
5. That I have not the cloudy winds to keep, [Sadness]
6. Fresh for the opening of the morning’s eye. [Sadness]
7. Such dim-conceived glories of the brain [Awe/Sublime]
8. Bring round the heart an indescribable feud; [Awe/Sublime]
9. So do these wonders a most dizzy pain, [Awe/Sublime]
10. That mingles Grecian grandeur with the rude Wasting of old Time—with a billowy main— [Awe/Sublime]
11. A sun—a shadow of a magnitude. [Awe/Sublime]

The overall effect of the stanza is one of a sense of wonder and awe, tempered by a sense of sadness and despair.
